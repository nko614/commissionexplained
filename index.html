Step 2

Why bigger targets don't mean more work

Think of it like this: if a subscription used to cost $100/month and now costs $133/month, each deal you close counts for more toward your target.

Let's say you sold 100 units before at the old price:
Old: 100 units × $1.00 = $100 revenue
New: 100 units × $1.33 = $133 revenue
Same effort, 33% more revenue — because each deal is worth more now.
Applied to your targets:
Old target: $1,300 MRR at old prices
New target: $2,100 MRR at new prices (+33%)
Effective volume needed: $2,100 ÷ 1.33 ≈ $1,579
The volume increase is modest, and the higher deal values more than compensate. Your payout math stays the same.

Common Questions

We hear you — let's clear it up

"My target went up 61%! That's not fair!"
The dollar target went up, but prices went up too. Each deal you close is now worth 33% more in revenue. You're not running faster on the treadmill — the treadmill is just counting in bigger steps. Your payout at every achievement % is identical.
"Why change anything if it's the same?"
When we raised prices, the targets needed to reflect the new revenue per deal. Otherwise, hitting 100% would have been too easy, the accelerators would kick in too early, and the plan economics wouldn't make sense long-term. This keeps everything calibrated correctly.
"Are my accelerators the same?"
Yes. The payout tiers and multipliers are recalibrated so that at every achievement level — 50%, 75%, 100%, 120%, 150%, 200% — your total commission is exactly the same dollar amount as the old plan.
"What if I was already close to hitting target under the old plan?"
Your existing pipeline deals will now close at the higher price point, so they'll count for more toward your new target. The ratio stays the same.
"Prove it."
Use the slider above. Pick any achievement level from 1% to 200%. The old plan payout and new plan payout are identical at every single level. That's not a coincidence — it's by design.
